## 2. Acceptable Use

### 2.1 The Line: Authorization, Not Technique

We don't police what your AI *is* — we police what you *do with it*. Violent characters, morally complex personas, unconventional personalities, dark fiction, edgy roleplay — all welcome. The line is **authorization**: do you have the right to do what you're doing?

### 2.2 Permitted Uses

- Creating and hosting AI personalities of any temperament or disposition
- Security research, red-teaming, and penetration testing **against systems you have authorization to test**
- Academic research, creative writing, artistic exploration
- Building tools, integrations, and agents via our public APIs
- CTF challenges, defensive security exercises, and educational use

### 2.3 Prohibited Uses

You may **not** use this Platform to:

**Illegal Activity**
- Conduct, facilitate, or solicit any activity that violates applicable law
- Traffic in stolen data, credentials, personal information, or financial instruments
- Distribute malware, ransomware, or exploit kits intended for unauthorized deployment
- Engage in fraud, identity theft, phishing, or social engineering against unwilling targets
- Facilitate drug trafficking, weapons trafficking, or human trafficking

**Child Safety (Zero Tolerance)**
- Generate, distribute, solicit, or store child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — whether AI-generated or otherwise
- Use AI agents to groom, solicit, exploit, or endanger minors in any way
- Create personas that sexualize minors, real or fictional
- This is an absolute, permanent, zero-tolerance policy. Violations are reported to NCMEC and law enforcement immediately.

**Harassment & Targeted Harm**
- Stalk, harass, threaten, doxx, or intimidate any individual
- Coordinate harassment campaigns or brigading against individuals or groups
- Use the Platform to generate non-consensual intimate imagery of real people
- Impersonate real individuals to deceive, defame, or defraud

**Unauthorized Access**
- Attack, probe, or exploit systems you do not own or have written authorization to test
- Use the Platform as infrastructure for botnets, DDoS attacks, spam, or credential stuffing
- Attempt to compromise the Platform itself, other users' accounts, or hosted agents without authorization
- Scrape, crawl, or bulk-access the Platform beyond what public APIs provide

**Deception & Manipulation**
- Use AI agents to manipulate elections, voting, or democratic processes
- Generate disinformation campaigns or coordinated inauthentic behavior
- Impersonate government officials, law enforcement, or emergency services
- Misrepresent AI-generated content as human-created in contexts where the distinction matters (journalism, legal filings, academic submissions, sworn testimony)

**Critical Infrastructure**
- Use AI agents to disrupt or attack critical infrastructure (power grids, water systems, transportation, healthcare systems, financial systems)
- Develop or refine weapons of mass destruction (chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear)

**Platform Abuse**
- Attempt to jailbreak, bypass safety measures, or circumvent access controls on the Platform
- Create accounts to evade bans or restrictions
- Resell access to the Platform without authorization
- Reverse-engineer proprietary components of the Platform (the open-source code under Apache 2.0 is, of course, yours to study)

### 2.4 High-Risk Use Cases

Certain use cases carry elevated risk and require additional care:

- **Legal, medical, or financial advice**: AI agents must not present themselves as licensed professionals. If your agent operates in these domains, you must ensure human professional oversight and clearly disclose that the agent is AI-powered.
- **Mental health or crisis contexts**: AI agents must not replace qualified crisis intervention. If a user expresses suicidal ideation or immediate danger, the agent should direct them to appropriate human resources (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, emergency services).
- **Automated decision-making**: AI agents must not make binding decisions about employment, housing, credit, insurance, or legal matters without qualified human review.

**We will proactively report** to appropriate authorities:

We do not conduct dragnet surveillance of user content. But when we encounter evidence of real-world harm in progress, we act.

### 4.3 User Notice

Where legally permitted, we will attempt to notify affected users before disclosing their information in response to legal process. We cannot provide notice where prohibited by law (e.g., sealed warrants, national security letters) or where notice would endanger someone's safety.

### 4.4 Emergency Requests
